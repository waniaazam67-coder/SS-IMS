1. Acceptance of Terms

By accessing the IMS, you agree to use it only for authorized internal work and to follow Company policies, assigned permissions, and instructions from authorized administrators.

2. Authorized Use

The IMS is an internal portal for Inventory Requests, Stock Management, Purchase Orders (PO), Goods Received Notes (GRN), Vendor Records, Approval Workflows, notifications, settings, and Audit Logs. Users may access only the functions and records required for their assigned role.

3. User Responsibilities

  • Use only your assigned official account and complete Email Verification before accessing the IMS.
  • Keep your password secure and use Password Reset only for your own official account.
  • Enter accurate Inventory Requests, stock quantities, PO details, GRN details, Vendor Records, approvals, and notes.
  • Verify item IDs, quantities, locations, vendors, prices, taxes, and dates before submitting or approving records.
  • Do not share passwords, Firebase tokens, invitation links, or account access.
  • Report incorrect records, unauthorized access, misuse, or security concerns promptly.
  • Sign out when using shared or public devices.

4. Administrator Responsibilities

  • Assign Role-Based Access Control permissions according to job responsibilities.
  • Review user access regularly and deactivate accounts that are no longer authorized.
  • Maintain locations, categories, roles, settings, and master data responsibly.
  • Review Audit Logs and investigate suspicious, unauthorized, or incorrect activity.
  • Ensure retention, backup, and account management follow Company policy.

5. Role-Based Permissions

IMS access is controlled through Role-Based Access Control. Permissions may include viewing inventory, managing stock, creating Inventory Requests, approving requests, issuing stock, managing POs, posting GRNs, managing Vendor Records, viewing Audit Logs, managing users, and changing settings. The Company may update permissions at any time to protect operations or correct access assignments.

All IMS records, including Inventory Requests, Stock Management records, Purchase Orders, GRNs, Vendor Records, Audit Logs, settings, and notifications, are Company business records. Users do not acquire ownership rights in IMS data by entering, editing, approving, exporting, or viewing it.

7. Inventory, PO, GRN, and Vendor Records

Inventory Requests and Stock Management records must reflect actual operational needs and stock activity. Purchase Orders must be created only for authorized procurement. GRNs must be posted only for goods or services actually received and verified. Vendor Records must be accurate, business-relevant, and maintained according to Company procurement procedures.

8. Approval Workflows

Approval Workflows document authorization decisions for Inventory Requests, stock issuance, transport requests, procurement, and related IMS activities. Approvers must review the available information before approving or rejecting a record. Approval actions are recorded in Audit Logs and may be used for internal review and accountability.

  • Submitting false, duplicate, misleading, or unauthorized Inventory Requests, stock movements, POs, GRNs, or Vendor Records.
  • Bypassing Email Verification, Password Reset controls, authentication, Approval Workflows, Audit Logs, or security controls.
  • Sharing credentials, Firebase tokens, invite links, or session access.
  • Entering malicious code, abusive content, or unrelated personal data into IMS fields.
  • Using, exporting, copying, or disclosing IMS data without authorization.
  • Interfering with system availability, performance, or integrity.
